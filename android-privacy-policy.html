TrashVPN Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 22, 2026

TrashVPN (“the App,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect information when you use TrashVPN.

By using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 VPN Service Information

TrashVPN is a VPN/network proxy application. To provide VPN connections, server synchronization, route selection, and connection status features, the App may process:

  • A client identifier generated by the App;
  • A public key used for encrypted server communication;
  • Server/node lists, server addresses, ports, and latency test results;
  • VPN connection status, selected node, route mode, and local settings;
  • Request time, app version, and basic network request information.

We do not intentionally read, record, or store your browsing content, messages, account passwords, payment information, or webpage content.

However, due to the technical nature of VPN services, your network traffic may pass through VPN or proxy servers. These servers may technically process necessary connection information, such as source IP address, destination address, connection time, and traffic volume. Such information is used only to provide, maintain, secure, and improve the service.

1.2 Device and App Information

To maintain app stability, troubleshoot errors, and provide updates, we may process:

  • App version, package name, and operating system version;
  • Crash logs, exception stack traces, thread names, and error messages;
  • Startup failure or runtime error information;
  • App update check information;
  • Network status information.

1.3 Advertising Information

The App may display advertisements, including Google AdMob ads and ads provided by our server.

Advertising features may process:

  • Ad impressions and click events;
  • Client identifier;
  • Click time;
  • Ad configuration, image URLs, and target URLs;
  • Information collected by Google AdMob according to Google’s own policies, such as advertising identifiers, device information, approximate location, and ad interaction data.

To provide version checks, update notifications, and APK downloads, the App may process:

  • Current app version;

  • Download verification information;
  • Installation status.

The App may request Android’s “Install unknown apps” permission only when you choose to install an APK through the in-app update feature.

1.5 Notification Permission

The App may request notification permission to show VPN running status, connection status, foreground service notifications, or important update reminders. You can disable notifications in your device settings.

We use information to:

  1. Provide VPN connections, server synchronization, route selection, and proxy services;
  2. Generate and maintain a client identifier for server allocation, quota management, and security verification;
  3. Perform server latency and availability tests;
  4. Display ads and record ad interactions;
  5. Check for updates, download update files, and verify file integrity;
  6. Diagnose crashes, startup failures, and runtime errors;
  7. Prevent abuse, attacks, abnormal requests, or misuse of service resources;
